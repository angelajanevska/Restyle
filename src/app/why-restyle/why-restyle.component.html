The fashion industry

Is the second-largest industry in the world and the second-largest polluter of the environment.

Prevent waste

End-users prefer throwing a lot of their used clothes in dumpsites and landfills, and the impact of such actions are not sustainable. Regardless of its size, no country in the world has the land space to sustain this waste practice.

Big problem is that very few items are actually recycled!

Only 15% of consumer-used textiles are recycled, whereas the remaining 85% end up discarded in landfills or incinerators.

Why sell your clothes?

Selling your unwanted clothes not only cleans your house and makes you cash. But prevents someone from buying new and helps lower the overall demand for new clothing to be produced.

By reducing demand for new clothing to be produced

We save natural resources and save the world from pollution. Because the production of every new item has an impact on the environment and pollutes in some way.

Pollution

Toxic chemicals are used to create synthetic dyes for our clothes. And they are often released into the environment and water streams. Transporting goods around the world emit tons of CO2 emissions into the atmosphere. And when things end up in landfills, greenhouse gases are emitted and chemicals are spread in the environment. This results in the pollution of groundwater and soil.

Energy and water are one of the world’s most precious resources!

The production of cotton clothes consumes a lot of water and energy. The production of a cotton t-shirt involves the use of about 2,700 liters of water. To produce material goods, we also need a lot of energy to have operating machines and factories. The transportation sector uses around one-fourth of the world’s total energy consumption.

Great for your wallet

This includes both the buyer and the seller. The buyer saves money, the seller receives revenue.
